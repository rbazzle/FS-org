## Roles & Responsibilities

### Director (Sponsor Director)
**Abbreviation:** None  
**Source:** SOC Charter  
**Definition:** Responsible for vision and strategic execution alignment. Accountable for aligning senior leadership vision with strategic execution in the assigned committee's outcome and ensuring cross-committee/homeland alignment.

**Key Functions:**
- Ensure business lead and committee align with organizational strategy
- Provide mentoring and leadership development
- Act as escalation point for issue resolution
- Identify decisions for steering committee

**Reports to:** Senior Leadership Team

---

### Strategic Objective Committee Lead (SOC Lead)
**Abbreviation:** SOC Lead  
**Source:** SOC Charter  
**Definition:** Responsible for leading committee activities and cross-discipline, operational strategy planning.

**Key Functions:**
- Manage operational strategies and execution plans
- Ensure committees collaborate effectively
- Synthesize reporting for stakeholders
- Provide input on investment decisions and resource prioritization

**Reports to:** Director

---

### Strategic Objective Program Manager
**Abbreviation:** PgM  
**Source:** SOC Charter  
**Definition:** Responsible for engineering orchestration, resource capacity awareness, governance and reporting.

**Key Functions:**
- Coordinate SOC weekly working sessions
- Facilitate monthly reporting to senior leadership
- Organize quarterly strategy sessions
- Contribute to cross-discipline orchestration

**Reports to:** SOC Lead

---

### Group Product Manager
**Abbreviation:** GPM  
**Source:** SOC Charter  
**Definition:** Responsible for product strategy, "Why and What", and alignment.

**Key Functions:**
- Primary liaison between product management and committee
- Coordinate organizational vision into actionable product strategies
- Lead product resource accountability and transparency
- Guide product roadmap development
- Represent non-technical stakeholder requirements

**Reports to:** Product Leadership / SOC Lead

---

### Group Engineering Manager
**Abbreviation:** GEM  
**Source:** SOC Charter  
**Definition:** Responsible for product engineering, "How and When", requirements.

**Key Functions:**
- Lead engineering execution alignment
- Represent capacity, dependencies, and sequencing
- Partner with Program Managers on delivery risks
- Lead technical feasibility and quality standards
- Report technical progress and blockers

**Reports to:** Engineering Leadership / SOC Lead

---

### Group Experience Manager
**Abbreviation:** GUXM  
**Source:** SOC Charter

## Objects & Artifacts

### Initiative
**Abbreviation:** None  
**Source:** SDLC Process Notes  
**Category:** Strategic Object  
**Definition:** A high-level objective set by leadership, often tied to business performance (e.g., increase revenue, improve retention).

**Purpose:** Provides strategic direction and context for product teams.

**Example:** "Improve customer retention"

**Answers:** "Why are we doing this?"

---

### Outcome
**Abbreviation:** None  
**Source:** SDLC Process Notes  
**Category:** Strategic Object  
**Definition:** A specific, observable change in customer behavior that derives from and supports the initiative.

**Types:**
- **Business Outcomes:** Lagging indicators (e.g., revenue) that teams can't directly control
- **Product Outcomes:** Leading indicators (e.g., increased usage) that teams can influence

**Purpose:** Shifts focus to impact (behavior) rather than output (features).

**Example:** "Increase repeat purchases by 15%"

**Answers:** "What change do we want?"

---

### Opportunity
**Abbreviation:** None  
**Source:** SDLC Process Notes  
**Category:** Discovery Object  
**Definition:** A gap between what customers expect and what they experience‚Äîpain points, desires, or unmet needs.

**Purpose:** Guides discovery and ideation by surfacing real customer problems.

**Application:**
- Discovered through interviews, usability tests, feedback
- Organized in Opportunity Solution Tree
- Prioritized based on impact on outcome

**Example:** "Customers abandon carts due to unclear shipping costs"

**Answers:** "Why aren't customers succeeding?"

---

### Solution
**Abbreviation:** None  
**Source:** SDLC Process Notes  
**Category:** Tactical Object  
**Definition:** A proposed feature, change, or experiment designed to solve a specific opportunity.

**Purpose:** Provides a tactical response to customer needs.

**Application:**
- Teams generate multiple ideas
- Use assumption testing to validate before building
- Prioritize based on feasibility, desirability, and impact

**Example:** "Add a shipping cost estimator to the cart page"

**Answers:** "What can we build to help?"

---

### WorkPlan
**Abbreviation:** None  
**Source:** SDLC Process Notes  
**Category:** Execution Object  
**Definition:** A time-boxed (‚â§90 days) plan that moves a validated solution through the phases of the Operating Model (SDLC).

**Purpose:** Operationalizes solutions and ensures delivery is time-bound and iterative.

**Phases:**
1. Idea Inception
2. Draft Proposal
3. Investigate
4. Build
5. Release & Measure

**Answers:** "How will we deliver and learn?"

---

### Opportunity Solution Tree
**Abbreviation:** OST  
**Source:** SDLC Process Notes  
**Category:** Discovery Tool  
**Definition:** A visual framework for organizing opportunities under outcomes, used to guide discovery and prioritization.

## Governance & Committees

### Strategic Objective Committee
**Abbreviation:** SOC  
**Source:** SOC Charter  
**Category:** Governance Structure  
**Definition:** Cross-functional committee organized around a specific strategic outcome, overseen by a director who is responsible for the committee's strategic outcome and accountable for its success.

**Purpose:** Unify vision from senior staff with strategy, organizational planning and delivery.

**Six Primary SOCs:**
1. Searchable Records
2. Ordinance-Ready Names
3. Submitters
4. RootsTech
5. Growth
6. Platform

---

### Steering Committee
**Abbreviation:** None  
**Source:** SOC Charter  
**Category:** Executive Governance  
**Definition:** Comprised of senior leadership team and business leads. Makes escalated decisions and provides strategic direction.

**Responsibilities:**
- Resource allocation discussions
- Strategic decision making
- Cross-committee alignment
