# FamilySearch Strategic Objective Committee Charter

## üéØ Purpose

FamilySearch Strategic Objective Committees (SOC) are structured to unify vision from senior staff with strategy, organizational planning and delivery through a cross-functional committee organized to, and responsible for the specific strategic outcome. Each committee is overseen by a director who is responsible for the committee's strategic outcome and is accountable for its success. This model provides the framework for unity, clarity, accountability, and agility‚Äîwithout prescription or dilution of strategic decision making.

## üß≠ Organizational Structure

### üîπ Strategic Objective Outcomes as Anchors

FamilySearch has the following strategic objective outcome designations:

- Searchable Records
- Ordinance-Ready Names
- Submitters
- RootsTech
- Growth
- Platform

### üîπ Committee Composition

Each SOC includes an assigned group of contributors. The Director assigns the Business Lead. Other senior leadership team members assign their respective staff participants to the committee. This ensures cross-discipline alignment throughout the organization. Each SOC is composed of:

- **Sponsor Director**
- **Strategic Objective Lead (SOC Lead)**
- **Strategic Objective Program Manager (PgM)**
- **Group Product Manager (GPM)**
- **Group Engineering Manager (GEM)**
- **Group Experience Manager (GUXM)**
- **Group Product Engagement Manager Lead (GPEM)**
- **Group Product Program Manager (GPgM)**
- **Business Intelligence Lead (BIL)**
- **Group QA Manager (GQAM)**
- **Other discipline contributors** as assigned (Homeland, QA, Data, Content, etc.)

This model is organized to foster clarity in roles and responsibilities, accountability for outcomes and delivery, and agility in adapting to discovery insights and shifting product and outcome priorities.

## üßë‚Äçüíº Role Descriptions

A summary of the different participants' roles is outlined below.

### üìã Director
*(Responsible for vision and strategic execution alignment)*

- Be accountable for aligning senior leadership vision with strategic execution in the assigned committee's outcome and ensure cross-committee/homeland alignment.
- Ensure the business lead and committee align with overall organizational strategy, perform responsibly, and run effectively.
- Provide mentoring and leadership development to business leaders.
- Assist with strategy oversight and definition.
- Provide guidance on resource allocation discussions between the committees and the Steering Committee.
- Ensure committees are working together effectively with the business lead.
- Act as an escalation point for issue resolution.
- Identify decisions to be escalated or made by the steering committee.

**Note:** The steering committee is comprised of the senior leadership team and business leads.

### üìã Strategic Objective Committee Lead
*(Responsible for leading committee activities and cross-discipline, operational strategy planning)*

- Manage and contribute to the definition and creation of operational strategies and plans for execution, ensuring they align to senior leadership's vision and associated outcome.
- Ensure the different Committees collaborate effectively.
- Organize and manage any Committee cross-functional sub-teams, which may include multiple discipline participants.
- Synthesize and facilitate reporting of operational and product outcomes for external stakeholders and the Senior Leadership Team.
- Ensure all available external and internal resources are being leveraged to achieve strategic objectives.
- Provide input on investment decisions and resource prioritization, including where to invest team's time to maximize impact; prioritizing alongside other committees to ensure strategic alignment, and present recommendations to senior leadership for discussion and ratification (electronically or in-person, as appropriate).
- Using data and Committee's learnings, recommend goals and business targets for annual planning.
- Lead the identification of risks and development of mitigation plans.

### üìã SOC Program Manager (PgM)
*(Responsible for engineering orchestration, resource capacity awareness, governance and reporting)*

- Coordinate SOC weekly working sessions, monthly reporting to senior leadership, and quarterly strategy sessions.
- Contribute to cross-discipline orchestration for activities of the SOC, working with other SOC leaders to foster collaboration and cross-SOC business activity.
- Responsible for working with SOC on reporting to Directors for accurate visibility into business outcomes.

### üìã Group Product Manager (GPM)
*(Responsible for product strategy, "Why and What", and alignment)*

- Serve as the primary liaison between product management and committee members, facilitating strategic product opportunity solutions, value proposition, desirability, and alignment to the SOC outcome.
- Coordinate translating organizational vision into actionable product strategies, collaborating with the committee members to ensure alignment with SOC outcomes.
- Lead in product resource accountability and transparency through resource coordination, KPI tracking, and reporting in collaboration with the committee.
- Lead product business review meeting cadence focused on product outcomes and strategic alignment.
- Contribute to competitive awareness aligned with committee-led strategic positioning.
- Guide product managers in developing and maintaining product roadmaps that reflect validated opportunities aligned with committee priorities.
- Provide product-level prioritization inputs in working sessions and business review recommendations, ensuring alignment with quarterly strategy and resource planning.
- Partner with Group Product Marketing Manager to obtain a deep understanding of customers and market dynamics, and to develop product positioning, product messaging, go-to-market plans, product update plans, and product retirement plans.
- Represent non-technical stakeholder requirements.

### üìã Group Engineering Manager (GEM)
*(Responsible for product engineering, "How and When", requirements)*

- Lead engineering execution alignment to Committee-approved initiative priorities in collaboration with the committee.
- Represent the committee's capacity, dependencies, and sequencing across engineering teams working on committee initiatives.
- Partner with Program Managers to identify and resolve capacity, delivery risks and blockers.
- Ensure initiative governance in continuous discovery and development processes follow standards and best practices.
- Coordinate engineering timelines and resource allocation with program managers and SOC Leads.
- Lead technical feasibility and quality standards outlook with committee, represent engineering perspectives in weekly working sessions, surfacing feasibility and risks.
- Report technical progress, blockers, and quality status in monthly business reviews.

### üìã Group Experience Manager (GUXM)
*(Responsible for experience validation, design standards and patron advocacy)*

- Bring patron experience insights into discovery, partnering with product management to ensure opportunities are validated by user experience desirability and accessibility.
- In close collaboration with product management, ensure that designs and solutions are usable, consistent, brand compliant and effective across FamilySearch's site.
- Provide Directors with experience-based tradeoffs (e.g., "This approach may deliver faster but risks lower adoption due to usability friction").
- Act as discipline steward, ensuring UX designers contribute effectively to team discovery and delivery while keeping work aligned to outcome goals.
- Ensure designs meet legal and global accessibility while meeting product requirements, experience insights, usability, accessibility, and associated tradeoffs.

### üìã Group QA Lead (GQAL)
*(Responsible for engineering quality assurance within teams and release readiness)*

- Oversee test execution and validation for initiatives.
- Ensures solutions meet quality standards before release (functional, performance, compliance).
- Partners with GEM on engineering quality standards and with Program Managers on production readiness checklists.
- Provides clear go/no-go signals based on QA validation results.
- Tracks and reports defects, test coverage, and quality metrics into governance forums.
- Ensure continuous improvement in QA practices across teams and initiatives.

### üìã Group Product Program Manager (GPgM)
*(Responsible for engineering orchestration, resource capacity awareness, governance and reporting)*

- Coordinate product timelines, milestones, and dependencies across engineering, product, UX, QA, and shared services.
- Provide delivery health updates in weekly working sessions, surfacing blockers and risks in weekly working sessions, monthly business reviews, and quarterly strategy sessions.
- Identify engineering resourcing constraints across teams and outcomes. Surface capacity issues in governance forums and recommend adjustments to sequencing or scope.
- Cross-discipline orchestration for launch activities across product, engineering, marketing, and support.
- Responsible for initiative checklist orchestration and governance so reporting to Directors has accurate visibility into delivery progress.
- Align sequencing and execution for initiatives that cut across multiple working groups (e.g., Record Access + Ordinance-Ready Names integration).
- Host dependency syncs and maintain cross-group delivery maps.
- Capture lessons learned post-release and feed into governance.

### üìã Group Product Engagement Manager
*(Responsible for understanding customer needs and wants, patron journeys, product positioning, messaging, go-to-market and retirement plans)*

- Develop a deep understanding of patron wants, needs and perceptions.
- Partner with product management in journey mapping for key customer segments (defined by the Committee) coordinating and coordinating with other GPMMs when customer segments cross SOC lines.
- Partner with the product manager to define product requirements that address researched and documented customer needs.
- Partner with product manager on product features, benefits, and positioning, ensuring audience messaging matches product capabilities.
- Create, inform, and align all key stakeholders on go-to-market plans, including audience, positioning, messaging, and communication plans.
- Partner with the SO Lead and Global Engagement team to ensure marketing/messaging activities support the broader department objectives.
- Partner with Group Experience Manager to ensure alignment to brand, track key KPIs (awareness, conversion, adoption, retention, satisfaction, etc.).
- Report results to the committee and recommend adjustments to strategies.
- Represent non-technical stakeholder requirements.

**Note:** Committee members are not permanent members of the committee. They can be rotated as determined by the overseeing Director or the corresponding senior staff members.

## üîß Product Functional Operation

### üîπ Working Sessions

- Focus on outcome requirements, organizational needs, product opportunities and potential solutions, approved initiative progress, blockers, metrics, and cross-functional committee collaboration.
- Evaluate and prioritize proposed opportunity solutions for Steering committee recommendations.
- Collaborate with topic experts and discipline specialists to present discovery learnings updates.
- Review journey and product roadmaps, approved initiative delivery timeframes and strategy outcomes.

### üîπ Monthly SOC Reporting

- SOC Lead presents the SOC report, with members of the committee, to the senior leadership team.
- Under the direction of the committee director, SOC Lead articulates strategic decisions and approvals.
- Report on outcome progress, initiative health, prioritization recommendations, and cross-committee dependencies.

### üîπ Quarterly Strategy Sessions

- The senior leadership refines vision and roadmaps with input from committees.
- Align cross-group initiatives and resolve resource conflicts.

## üìä Product Governance Cadence

- **Product Business Review:** GPM facilitates the review for evaluating and prioritized recommendations for new product opportunity solutions, existing product initiative learnings, product cross-committee considerations and surfaces gaps or strategic risks with the SOC Lead and the committee members. The committee makes recommendations for engineering initiative advancement, as prescribed in the SDLC, to engineering director leaders.

- **Weekly Product Delivery Coordination Report:** GEM facilitates this operational checkpoint review for initiative tracking, delivery timelines, and the resolution of execution blockers across teams. The appropriate program manager provides the reporting on each specific initiative.

## üìà Product Success Measures

- Achievement of strategic objective outcome KPI(s).
- Velocity and throughput of initiatives.
- Stakeholder clarity and satisfaction.
- Removal of cross-team friction and duplication.
- Continuous improvement and learning in discovery and delivery practices.
- Alignment with Senior staff in vision, visibility, and engagement in strategic decisions.

## üß© Summary

This charter defines a model that aligns initiative delivery with strategic business outcomes by empowering Strategic Objective Committees to steward execution, metrics, and discovery insights‚Äîunder the direction of senior leadership. It ensures vertical and horizontal coordination, enables unique and complimentary role collaboration, supports adaptive prioritization, and promotes transparency through shared tools and practices.
